About

Hello! And welcome again to my website! I am in my third year at the University of South Carolina studying music performance in French horn with JD Shaw and have minor in mathematics. I am currently doing a few research projects in a variety of areas. First, I am working on creating and fine-tuning this website with Dr. Reginald Bain for Projects in Computer Music. I am learning how to read and use HTML5 through tutorials and hope to learn other programs, like JavaScript, to enhance my knowledge in website design.

I am working on the Magellan Explorer with Patrick McFaddin, a visiting mathematics professor; we are researching if flipped learning impacts students' understanding on the applicability of mathematics and if it influences their learning and reasoning. This website provides a variety of resources and information on flipped learning and examples of flipped classrooms.

Lastly, I am a finalist for the Creativity in Music Awards, in which I will conduct an arts integration lesson with a middle school mathematics class. My plan is to analyze a piece of music for horn and piano, teach three math mini-lessons that correspond with the music I analyzed, and perform the piece at the end of the presentation. My advisor for this project is Dr. David Cutler.
